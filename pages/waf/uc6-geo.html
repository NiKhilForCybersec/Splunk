WAF-UC6: Geographic Anomaly Detection

Spike from countries that rarely access your services, or significant traffic from high-risk regions. Requires baseline lookup for comparison.

Severity MEDIUM
MITRE T1190
Schedule Every 15 min
Time Range Last 1 hour

Step 1: Decision Tree (Detection Logic)

flowchart TD
    START[Aggregate WAF blocks by source country] --> A[Lookup country baseline from lookup table]
    A --> B{Is country on high_risk list}
    B -->|Yes| C{Is attack count GT 50}
    C -->|Yes| HIGHRISK[HIGH RISK COUNTRY with significant volume]
    C -->|No| MONITOR1[High risk but low volume - monitor]
    B -->|No| D[Calculate deviation from baseline]
    D --> D1[deviation = current minus expected divided by expected times 100]
    D1 --> E{Is deviation GT 300 percent}
    E -->|Yes| F{Is attack count GT 100}
    F -->|Yes| MAJOR[Major Geographic Anomaly - 3x baseline]
    F -->|No| MODERATE[Moderate Anomaly - volume low despite deviation]
    E -->|No| G{Is deviation GT 200 percent AND attacks GT 50}
    G -->|Yes| MODERATE
    G -->|No| NORMAL[Within expected range - no alert]
    HIGHRISK --> CRIT[Severity based on high risk country]
    MAJOR --> HIGH1[HIGH severity]
    MODERATE --> MED[MEDIUM severity]
    CRIT --> CREATE[Create Notable Event]
    HIGH1 --> CREATE
    MED --> CREATE
    CREATE --> ENRICH[Enrich with attacker IPs from that country]
    ENRICH --> THROTTLE[Throttle by country for 1 hour]

Prerequisite: Create Country Baseline Lookup

Step 0: Generate Baseline (Run Once, Then Schedule Weekly)

This detection REQUIRES a baseline lookup. Run this query to create it, then schedule it to refresh weekly:

```Run this to create baseline lookup (30 days of data)
index=akamai sourcetype=akamai:siem earliest=-30d
| spath
| eval waf_action=coalesce(action, mvindex('attackData.rules{}.ruleAction',0))
| eval country=coalesce(src_country,'geo.country')
| where waf_action="deny" OR waf_action="block"
| where isnotnull(country) AND country!=""
| stats count as total_attacks BY country
| eval daily_avg = round(total_attacks / 30, 0)
| eval hourly_avg = round(daily_avg / 24, 0)
| eval hourly_avg = if(hourly_avg < 1, 1, hourly_avg)
| eval is_high_risk = if(country IN ("CN","RU","KP","IR","BY","VN","NG","PK"), "true", "false")
| table country daily_avg hourly_avg is_high_risk
| outputlookup country_baseline.csv
Schedule This Query
Save this as a scheduled report that runs weekly (e.g., Sunday at 2 AM) to keep the baseline current. Old baselines will miss new attack patterns.

High-Risk Country Reference

Country CodeCountryReason for High-RiskAction
CN China High volume of scanning, APT activity Alert on any significant volume
RU Russia State-sponsored attacks, ransomware groups Alert on any significant volume
KP North Korea State-sponsored APT (Lazarus, etc.) Alert on ANY traffic
IR Iran State-sponsored attacks Alert on any significant volume
BY Belarus Often used as proxy for RU activity Higher scrutiny threshold

Note: Customize this list based on your organization's business relationships. If you do business in these countries, you may need different thresholds.

Step A: Build SPL in Search First

Where: Splunk Search → Build and test SPL

Variant A: Basic Geographic Anomaly with Baseline

index=akamai sourcetype=akamai:siem earliest=-1h
| spath
| eval waf_action=coalesce(action, mvindex('attackData.rules{}.ruleAction',0))
| eval country=coalesce(src_country,'geo.country')
| eval src_ip=coalesce(src_ip,'attackData.clientIP')
| where waf_action="deny" OR waf_action="block"
| where isnotnull(country) AND country!=""
| stats count as attacks 
        dc(src_ip) as unique_attackers 
        values(src_ip) as sample_ips
        BY country
| lookup country_baseline.csv country OUTPUT hourly_avg is_high_risk
| eval expected = if(isnotnull(hourly_avg), hourly_avg, 1)
| eval deviation_pct = round((attacks - expected) / expected * 100, 0)
| where (deviation_pct > 200 AND attacks > 50) OR (is_high_risk="true" AND attacks > 50)
| eval anomaly_type = case(
    is_high_risk="true" AND attacks > 100, "High Risk Country - Significant Volume",
    is_high_risk="true", "High Risk Country - Moderate Volume",
    deviation_pct > 300 AND attacks > 100, "Major Geographic Anomaly",
    1=1, "Moderate Geographic Anomaly"
)
| eval severity = case(
    is_high_risk="true" AND attacks > 100, "high",
    deviation_pct > 300 AND attacks > 100, "high",
    1=1, "medium"
)
| table country attacks expected deviation_pct unique_attackers is_high_risk anomaly_type sample_ips severity
| sort - deviation_pct

Variant B: With Target Analysis

Use when: You want to know what the traffic from anomalous countries is targeting. 

index=akamai sourcetype=akamai:siem earliest=-1h
| spath
| eval waf_action=coalesce(action, mvindex('attackData.rules{}.ruleAction',0))
| eval country=coalesce(src_country,'geo.country')
| eval src_ip=coalesce(src_ip,'attackData.clientIP')
| eval uri=coalesce(uri,'httpMessage.requestUri')
| eval vhost=coalesce(vhost,'httpMessage.requestHeaders.Host')
| where waf_action="deny" OR waf_action="block"
| where isnotnull(country) AND country!=""
| stats count as attacks 
        dc(src_ip) as unique_attackers 
        dc(uri) as unique_targets
        dc(vhost) as unique_hosts
        values(vhost) as target_hosts
        values(uri) as sample_targets
        BY country
| lookup country_baseline.csv country OUTPUT hourly_avg is_high_risk
| eval expected = if(isnotnull(hourly_avg), hourly_avg, 1)
| eval deviation_pct = round((attacks - expected) / expected * 100, 0)
| where (deviation_pct > 200 AND attacks > 50) OR (is_high_risk="true" AND attacks > 50)
| eval target_pattern = case(
    unique_targets < 5, "Focused Attack - specific targets",
    unique_targets < 20, "Semi-Targeted",
    1=1, "Spray Attack - scanning"
)
| eval severity = case(is_high_risk="true" AND attacks > 100, "high", deviation_pct > 300, "high", 1=1, "medium")
| table country attacks expected deviation_pct unique_attackers unique_targets target_pattern target_hosts sample_targets is_high_risk severity

Step C: Handle False Positives

Common False Positives & Solutions

FP SourceHow to IdentifySolution
New international customers Business expansion into new country coincides with spike Update baseline more frequently during expansion; coordinate with business
Marketing campaign Spike correlates with marketing push in specific region Coordinate with marketing; temporarily adjust thresholds for campaign region
VPN exit nodes Traffic shows as country X but users are actually elsewhere Cross-reference with known VPN provider IP ranges; may need separate handling
Cloud provider location Traffic from country where cloud data center is located AWS/GCP/Azure data center IPs may geolocate to unexpected countries
Stale baseline Baseline doesn't reflect recent legitimate traffic patterns Refresh baseline more frequently; check baseline age
