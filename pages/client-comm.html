Client Communication Guide

Email templates, meeting prep, and professional response patterns

Communication Principles

Be Specific

Provide concrete timelines, specific actions, and clear deliverables. Avoid vague language.

Own the Problem

Never say "I don't know" without a follow-up plan. Take ownership and provide next steps.

Set Expectations

Under-promise and over-deliver. Give realistic timelines with buffer for unknowns.

  • "That's not my area"
  • "I think maybe..."
  • "It should work..."
  • "Someone needs to..."

‚úÖ Say Instead

  • "Let me research this and get back to you by [time]"
  • "I'll coordinate with [team] to resolve this"
  • "Based on the data, I recommend..."
  • "I've tested this configuration and confirmed..."
  • "I'll take ownership of [task] and update you by [date]"

Meeting Best Practices

Before the Meeting

Review progress since last meeting. Prepare 2-3 key points to cover. Have SPL queries ready to demo. Anticipate questions.

Start of Meeting

State the agenda upfront. "Today I'll cover: progress update, demo of UC1, and discuss blockers." Respect their time.

During the Meeting

Be concise. Show working demos, not just slides. When asked something you don't know, write it down and commit to following up.

End of Meeting

Summarize action items with owners and dates. "To confirm: I'll have UC2 ready by Friday, and [Name] will provide the user list by Wednesday."
